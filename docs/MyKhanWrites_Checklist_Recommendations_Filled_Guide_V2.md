## 0. What changed from the V1 filled guide

1. ⚠️ **Genre label.** "Cozy literary fiction" demoted from public label to internal-only note.
   It contradicts "warm stories with **cold corners**" and risks mislabeling the book. Publicly:
   **describe the feeling, don't slap a genre on it.** If a label is required (retailer metadata,
   pitching), use **"literary fiction"**; "up-lit" / "book-club fiction" are the closest warm-but-sad fits.
2. ⚠️ **Analytics.** GA4 → **Plausible** (or self-hosted Umami). A cookie-consent banner fights the
   calm, non-corporate first impression. Plausible needs no banner and still tracks your events.
3. ⚠️ **Author hero image.** Flip the distinctiveness: **author hero = typographic / spacious**,
   **novel hero = the rich literal scene.** The generic writer's-desk should not be the image that
   represents the *author*.
4. ⚠️ **Trust signals.** "Current screenplay in progress" removed from the *trust* list (unfinished
   work isn't validation) and moved to *range*.
5. ⚠️ **Homepage order.** Locked to the tighter **6-block** order; the standalone "Author Identity"
   section from brief §6 is **retired** (it stated the identity three times on one page).
6. ⚠️ **About bio.** Stop opening the bio by repeating the positioning slogan verbatim. The slogan
   appears **once** (hero). The bio opens with a **fact**.
7. ⚠️ **Primary CTA framing.** Keep a book-led entry, but the list identity + welcome email must
   **bridge to the author**, so the list survives book one. Confirm the "early pages" asset exists.
8. ➕ **New decisions added:** immersive-site sequencing/fallback, contact-form backend, AI-image
   tell cleanup, mobile hero behavior, launch-minimum scope.

## 10. Visual System

- ✅ **Style:** dreamlike semi-realism, cinematic touches, warm dusk with quiet noir beneath; typographic restraint over full immersion.
- 🔵 **Palette:** deep charcoal, warm parchment, muted brown, soft gold/amber, rain-grey; **subtle yellow only for novel-specific accents.** Avoid neon, harsh red, corporate blue, sweet pastels, generic café browns.
- 🔵 **Fonts:** literary serif for headings, clean readable sans for body, optional handwritten accent only for tiny details.
- ✅ **Animation:** quietly alive, strict ceiling — soft fades, gentle reveals, restrained hover, reduced-motion fallback. No parallax, scroll-jacking, glitch, animated cursor, autoplay video, typewriter-everywhere. **Do not make mug-steam the hero's signature motion.**
- 🔵 **Mug:** stays *in the image* as an incidental object; never a logo/icon/section motif.
- ➕ **AI-image tell cleanup [CONFIRM]:** if any hero is AI-generated, retouch tells (melted text, uncanny reflections, over-perfect bokeh, impossible geometry) before use. A hero that *looks* AI undercuts the whole "not AI-polished" goal.
- ➕ **Mobile hero [CONFIRM]:** specify how the atmosphere + text behave at ~380px (text-first; image as background or below). Mood-heavy heroes routinely collapse on phones.

**Novel-page hero image:** ✍️/🔵 see the dedicated prompt in `MyKhanWrites_Next_Session_Pack.md`.

- ✅ **Architecture:** one master audience + tags. (This *is* Kit's native model — there are no separate "lists" in Kit, so this is the path of least resistance.)

- ➕ **Must be true on both sites:** one Kit account; **every form applies a `source:` tag**; one consistent incentive; GDPR-aware consent (double opt-in recommended for clean deliverability).
- 🔵 **Subscribers receive:** the opening pages now; then cover, launch date, early chapters; occasional letters from the desk.
- 🔵 **Frequency:** occasional, heavier around launch.
- 🔵 **Signup copy (gift-first, author-bridged):**
  > **Read the first pages of *They Only Came When It Rained***
  > I'm finishing my first novel. Subscribe and I'll send you the opening pages now — then the cover, the launch date, and the occasional letter from the desk where the next stories take shape.
  > *No spam. Leave whenever you like.*
- ➕ **Welcome email = the incentive. Build this asset before launch:**
  > **Subject: Here are the first pages**
