# After the Script — Your Path to Screen Representation

**For:** Meher Yar Khan · **Date:** 2026-06-25 · **Status:** reference roadmap (research-backed)
**Visual version:** `docs/MyKhanWrites_Screenwriting_Roadmap.html`

> **The honest frame first.** Your website is **~10% of this** — a credibility backstop, *not* how reps
> find you. No authoritative source lists a personal website as a discovery channel. The other **90%**
> is the script being undeniably great, plus the real paths below. Your debut-novel credential is a
> genuine, rare piece of validation — lead with it everywhere.

---

## Phase 0 — Protect it first (BEFORE anything goes public)

Do this before the `/screen` page publishes or you send the script to anyone.

- [ ] **Register with the US Copyright Office** (~$45–65, electronic, [copyright.gov](https://www.copyright.gov/registration/)). This is the *only* protection with legal teeth — registration is required before you can sue, with statutory damages up to $150,000 for willful infringement. **Mandatory.**
- [ ] **(Optional) WGA Registry** (~$10–25, renews every 5 years) — a timestamped proof-of-authorship for credit disputes. A *supplement*, not a substitute for copyright.
- [ ] **Skip "poor man's copyright"** (mailing yourself a copy) — the Copyright Office says it provides no protection. Legally worthless.
- [ ] **Gate the script** — share a watermarked PDF *on request* only. Never a public download.

## Phase 1 — Make the script undeniable

- [ ] Polish to a "couldn't put it down" level. **The script is the real gate** — one top manager requests ~80 of 100 query scripts but is impressed by *less than one a week*. Access isn't the bottleneck; the writing is.
- [ ] (Over time) build toward **2–3 polished scripts** — most reps want to see more than one sample before signing. One feature makes the near-term value *credibility*, while you grow the slate.

## Phase 2 — Build your professional presence

- [ ] **Set up IMDbPro** — closer to mandatory than a personal site; it's the "online resume" reps check.
- [ ] **The `/screen` page on this site** (we'll build it): logline · short synopsis · themes · comparables ("in the vein of *X* meets *Y*") · status ("completed feature — seeking representation") · bio leading with the novel credential · a *separate* industry contact path. Framed as "Selected Work" so it can grow.

**What to put online vs. withhold:**

| ✅ Do put online | ❌ Don't put online |
|---|---|
| Logline (1–2 sentences) | The full screenplay as a public PDF/download |
| Short synopsis (longer on request) | A direct, ungated link to the script |
| Genre · tone · themes | A cold "click here to read my screenplay" ask |
| Comparables ("in the vein of…") | Large excerpts that reconstruct the script |
| Status: "Completed — read on request" | Anything before US Copyright is filed |
| Pro contact route + IMDb link | Anything implying it's been widely shopped |

## Phase 3 — Pursue representation (the real 90%)

In rough order of power:

1. [ ] **Referrals (strongest).** An intro from anyone in publishing/film goes to the top of the pile. Use every contact your novel and network give you.
2. [ ] **Competitions / fellowships the industry watches** — Austin Film Festival, ScreenCraft, PAGE, Final Draft Big Break; **Academy Nicholl** (now via partner programs only — e.g., Austin FF / WGF / USC).
3. [ ] **The Black List** ([blcklst.com](https://blcklst.com)) — a rare **8+ score** gets you circulated to reps/execs. Useful, not a plan by itself.
4. [ ] **Targeted queries to *managers* and junior agents** (not top agents first). Find them via **IMDbPro** and **It's on the Grid**. A query = logline + genre + one-paragraph synopsis + any validation (placements, the novel).

> **2026 notes:** Coverfly shut down (Aug 2025) — its role moved to FilmFreeway, the Black List, ISA, and Script Revolution. Nicholl is now partner-programs-only (no direct submissions).

## How the website fits

The site's job is to **not lose** a warm lead, not to generate one. When a rep you've queried (or who saw your contest placement) Googles you, the `/screen` page + IMDbPro make you look like a serious, professional, *finishing* writer with a coherent voice — and make your logline and contact effortless to grab. That's the whole job. Build it well, then spend your real energy on Phases 0–3.
