After the Script — your path to representation

A research-backed roadmap for turning your completed feature into representation, and where this website actually fits.

10%

The honest frame. Your website is about 10% of this — a credibility backstop, not how reps find you. No authoritative source lists a personal website as a discovery channel. The other 90% is the script being undeniable, plus the real paths below. Your debut-novel credential is genuine, rare validation — lead with it everywhere.

0

Protect it first

Before anything goes public, or you send the script anywhere
  • Register with the US Copyright Office (~$45–65, electronic). The only protection with legal teeth — required before you can sue, statutory damages up to $150,000. Mandatory.
  • (Optional) WGA Registry (~$10–25, renews every 5 years) — a timestamp for credit disputes. A supplement, not a substitute.
  • Skip "poor man's copyright" (mailing yourself a copy) — the Copyright Office says it provides no protection. Worthless.
  • Gate the script — share a watermarked PDF on request only. Never a public download.
1

Make the script undeniable

The script is the real gate
  • Polish to a "couldn't put it down" level. One top manager requests ~80 of 100 query scripts but is impressed by less than one a week — access isn't the bottleneck, the writing is.
  • Over time, build toward 2–3 polished scripts — most reps want more than one sample. One feature makes the near-term value credibility while you grow the slate.
2

Build your professional presence

The brochure + showcase
  • Set up IMDbPro — closer to mandatory than a personal site; the "online resume" reps check.
  • The /screen page (we'll build it): logline · synopsis · themes · comparables · status ("completed feature — seeking representation") · bio leading with the novel credential · a separate industry contact path. Framed as "Selected Work" so it can grow.

3

Pursue representation

The real 90% — in order of power
  1. Referrals (strongest). An intro from anyone in publishing/film goes to the top of the pile. Use every contact your novel and network give you.
  2. Competitions / fellowships the industry watches — Austin Film Festival, ScreenCraft, PAGE, Final Draft Big Break; Academy Nicholl (now via partner programs only).
  3. The Black List (blcklst.com) — a rare 8+ score gets you circulated. Useful, not a plan by itself.
  4. Targeted queries to managers + junior agents (not top agents first). Find them via IMDbPro and It's on the Grid. Query = logline + genre + one-paragraph synopsis + validation.
2026 notes: Coverfly shut down (Aug 2025) — its role moved to FilmFreeway, the Black List, ISA, and Script Revolution. Nicholl is now partner-programs-only.

How the website fits

The site's job is to not lose a warm lead, not to generate one. When a rep you've queried — or who saw your contest placement — Googles you, the /screen page + IMDbPro make you look like a serious, finishing writer with a coherent voice, and make your logline and contact effortless to grab. That's the whole job. Build it well, then spend your real energy on Phases 0–3.
