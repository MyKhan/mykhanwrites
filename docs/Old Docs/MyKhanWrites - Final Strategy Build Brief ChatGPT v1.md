## 1. Executive Summary

MyKhanWrites.com should be a focused, elegant, emotionally intelligent author website for **Meher Yar Khan**.

The site should not behave like:

- a commercial services website
- a generic author blog
- a cluttered archive
- a portfolio of every project
- a duplicate of the immersive novel website

It should behave like:

> A serious author home that introduces Meher Yar Khan, supports the upcoming novel launch, and quietly signals a broader future across fiction, screen, and other story worlds.

The corrected strategic direction is:

> **A clear, human author homepage with atmosphere — not an atmospheric moodboard with some author information attached.**

The site must first help visitors understand, then help them feel, then invite them to act.

---

## 2. Core Strategic Tension

The main tension is this:

- The current novel is the immediate launch priority.
- MyKhanWrites must remain a long-term author identity hub.

If the site leans too heavily toward the novel, it becomes a book landing page and loses its author-hub role.

If it leans too heavily toward abstract author identity, it becomes vague, pretty, and low-converting.

### Final Position

The site should use a **hybrid structure**:

- Homepage hero introduces Meher as an author and references the current novel.
- The current novel is strongly visible but does not consume the whole identity.
- The immersive novel website remains the deeper book-world experience.
- MyKhanWrites remains the author home.

### Reasoning

Visitors need orientation quickly. They should not be sent away before they understand whose site they are on and why the author matters. At the same time, the novel is the live opportunity, so it must be visible above the fold.

## 3. Author Positioning

### Final Direction

The author should not be positioned by genre alone.

Meher is not only a cozy literary fiction novelist. His broader work includes or may include:

- cozy literary fiction
- neo-noir
- psychological thriller
- science fiction
- fantasy
- screenwriting
- interactive or game-connected story worlds
- software tools for writers

Therefore, the author identity should be built around recurring emotional and dramatic territory, not one genre label.

### Core Identity Layers

**Deep identity:**  
The Human Contradiction Writer

**Public tone:**  
The Literary Shadow-Observer

**Current doorway:**  
The Warm Worldbuilder

### Preferred Humanized Positioning Direction

> Meher Yar Khan writes warm stories with cold corners — fiction about lonely people, hidden selves, and the places they go to feel less alone.

### Alternate Positioning Lines to Test

1. **Warmer / more human:**  
   Meher Yar Khan writes warm stories with cold corners — fiction about lonely people, hidden selves, and the places they go to feel less alone.

2. **More literary / serious:**  
   Meher Yar Khan writes intimate, atmospheric stories about belonging, hidden selves, and the quiet darkness beneath ordinary lives.

3. **Broader / cinematic:**  
   Meher Yar Khan writes fiction and screen stories about lonely people, hidden selves, and worlds that comfort, haunt, or transform them.

### Reasoning

The earlier line — “literary and cinematic stories about loneliness, identity, and morally complicated people caught between comfort and darkness” — was strategically useful but sounded too abstract and AI-polished.

The revised positioning keeps the same strategic spine but makes it more human, concrete, and memorable.

### Important Guardrail

Avoid sounding like a literary brand generator.

Use fewer abstract nouns. Use more concrete emotional images.

## 4. Emotional Tone

### Final Direction

The emotional tone remains:

> **Warm dusk with quiet noir beneath it.**

But the earlier 60/30/10 ratio should be treated as an internal design guide, not something rigid or scientific.

### Practical Translation

The site should feel:

- warm
- reflective
- literary
- intimate
- calm
- slightly shadowed
- serious but not grim
- personal but not casual
- polished but not sterile

It should not feel:

- too cozy-café
- too crime-thriller
- too gothic
- too corporate
- too tech-founder
- too abstract
- too self-important

### Reasoning

Meher’s current book is cozy literary fiction, but his broader creative direction includes morally complex and darker work. The tone needs to support both without misrepresenting either.

The homepage should lead with warmth and humanity. The darker undertone can emerge through wording, contrast, visuals, and the Writing/About sections.

## 5. Homepage Strategy

### Final Recommendation

Use a **hybrid homepage hero**.

The hero should communicate:

1. This is Meher Yar Khan’s author site.
2. He writes warm, reflective stories with deeper shadows.
3. A current novel is nearing launch.
4. Visitors can either join the author list or enter the novel world.

### Recommended Above-the-Fold Priorities

1. Author name
2. Clear author-positioning headline
3. Current novel mention
4. Primary CTA connected to subscription or launch list
5. Secondary CTA to immersive novel site
6. Atmospheric visual

### CTA Strategy

The earlier hero CTA was too heavily weighted toward sending visitors away:

> Enter the Novel World

That is still useful, but it should probably be a secondary CTA on MyKhanWrites.

### Recommended CTA Structure

**Primary CTA:**  
Join the Author List / Join the Launch List / Receive Author Letters

**Secondary CTA:**  
Enter the Novel World / Visit the Novel Site

**Optional smaller link:**  
Meet the Author

### Reasoning

The homepage should not make the visitor leave before they understand the author. The main goal of MyKhanWrites should be to build a lasting reader relationship, while still giving the novel site a strong pathway.

## 7. Novel Strategy

### Final Direction

The novel should be strongly present on MyKhanWrites, but the deeper experience should remain on the immersive novel site.

The Novel page on MyKhanWrites should be a:

> **Bridge page with teaser atmosphere.**

### It Should Include

- title or working title
- launch window
- one-sentence premise
- short emotional description
- book-specific visual
- CTA to immersive novel site
- optional CTA to novel-specific mailing list

### It Should Avoid

- duplicating the immersive website
- full lore explanations
- long excerpts
- too many motifs
- deep character details
- competing with the novel site’s main conversion path

### Reasoning

The immersive novel site exists to carry the full book-world experience. MyKhanWrites should introduce, orient, and route visitors.

## 8. Newsletter Strategy

### Final Direction

Use **two audience paths**, not necessarily one merged list.

### Audience Path 1: MyKhanWrites Author List

For people interested in Meher as a writer beyond one book.

Possible promise:

> Notes on fiction, drafts, story worlds, and the road to publication.

### Audience Path 2: Novel-Specific List

For people interested specifically in the upcoming novel and its world.

Possible promise:

> Updates, reveals, and launch news for the upcoming novel.

### Technical Recommendation

Use the same email platform if possible, but separate the audiences with:

- lists
- tags
- segments
- source tracking

### Cross-Invitation Strategy

Novel-list subscribers can later be invited to join the broader author list if they want future project updates.

Author-list subscribers can receive important novel launch updates without becoming trapped in only novel-specific emails.

### Reasoning

The relationship contract is different.

Someone who subscribes to the author site is saying:

> I am interested in this writer.

Someone who subscribes to the novel site may only be saying:

> I am interested in this book.

Those should not be treated as identical psychological commitments.

---

## 9. Newsletter Copy Guardrail

The earlier “sent quietly, when there is something worth saying” phrasing was tasteful but too apologetic.

### Revised Direction

The newsletter should still feel literary and restrained, but it must give people a clear reason to subscribe.

Possible author-list copy:

> Receive occasional notes from Meher Yar Khan on fiction, drafts, story worlds, and the long road from first idea to finished book.

Possible stronger launch-aware copy:

> I’m preparing my upcoming novel for launch. Join the author list for book news, behind-the-scenes notes, and occasional letters from the desk where the stories take shape.

### Reasoning

A newsletter is a core pre-launch asset. It should not be buried or described defensively.

## 10. Visual Identity

### Final Direction

The visual identity remains:

> Warm room, dark window, lamplight, unfinished pages, subtle mug, and an origami paper crane.

### The Mug Decision

The mug stays because Meher likes the generated hero images and the mug supports introspection, reflection, and human ritual.

However:

- the mug should not become the brand
- it should not appear everywhere
- it should not look like café branding
- it should remain an atmospheric object

### The Paper Crane Decision

The paper crane remains an author-wide motif.

It can symbolize:

- fragility
- memory
- transformation
- story-making
- handmade craft
- quiet hope
- folding rough material into form

### The Yellow Raincoat Decision

The little girl in the yellow raincoat should remain mostly novel-specific.

It may appear:

- on the Novel page
- in novel-specific imagery
- on the immersive site
- as a subtle hint, if needed

It should not become a general MyKhanWrites motif.

### Reasoning

The mug is familiar and emotionally accessible. The paper crane is more distinctive and symbolic. The yellow raincoat is powerful but too tied to one book to carry the entire author brand.

## 12. Writing Page Strategy

### Final Direction

Use three categories:

- Fiction
- Screen
- Other Worlds

### Reasoning

“Future Worlds” sounded too grand and slightly unfinished. “Other Worlds” is softer, more literary, and less overpromising.

### Category Intent

**Fiction**  
For novels and prose work, including literary, cozy, speculative, noir, fantasy, and science fiction.

**Screen**  
For screenplays, psychological thrillers, cinematic work, and future screen stories.

**Other Worlds**  
For interactive narrative, possible game-connected work, immersive story experiments, and projects that do not fit cleanly into book or screen formats.

### Guardrail

Do not list every unfinished project at launch.

The page should present creative territory, not a messy archive.

### Include

- current novel status
- broader creative range

- novel nearly complete

## 16. Technical Strategy

### Final Direction

Use:

- Astro
- Tailwind CSS
- minimal JavaScript
- mostly static pages
- CSS-first animation
- optimized images
- optional MDX later
- Hostinger deployment

### Reasoning

MyKhanWrites is a content-led author site, not a complex software application. It should be fast, elegant, and maintainable.

Astro + Tailwind is appropriate because it gives custom design control without requiring a heavy React app.

### Avoid

- Hostinger AI builder
- generic WordPress theme
- heavy page builders
- unnecessary backend
- video hero
- complex CMS at launch
- overengineered interactivity

---

## 17. Animation Strategy

### Final Direction

The site should feel:

> Quietly alive, with a strict ceiling.

Allowed:

- subtle steam movement
- soft lamp glow
- gentle fade-ins
- paper-like card hover
- subtle section reveals

Avoid:

- heavy parallax
- scroll-jacking
- animated cursor
- glitch effects
- dramatic noir transitions
- typewriter effects everywhere
- autoplay video

### Accessibility Requirement

All non-essential motion should respect reduced-motion preferences.

### Reasoning

Motion should deepen the atmosphere, not become the user’s main memory of the site.
