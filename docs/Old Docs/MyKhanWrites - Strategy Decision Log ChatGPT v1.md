# MyKhanWrites.com Strategy Decision Log

**Project:** MyKhanWrites.com  
**Purpose:** Personal author/writing website for Meher Yar Khan  
**Prepared for:** Independent review and feedback  
**Date:** 2026-06-14  

---

## 1. Core Role of the Website

### Decision

MyKhanWrites.com will be the **author identity hub** for Meher Yar Khan.

It will not be the main commercial services site, and it will not duplicate the immersive website for the upcoming novel.

### Reasoning

The user already has a broader ecosystem:

- An immersive standalone website for the upcoming cozy literary fiction novel
- MyKhanWrites.com as the personal author hub
- ManTalksMedia.com as the commercial/services hub
- YouTube, apps, and other projects that may exist but should not clutter the author site

The strategic risk was that MyKhanWrites could become a confusing archive of every creative, technical, commercial, and media project. That would weaken the author identity.

The site’s primary job should be to make the author feel real, serious, polished, and memorable, while directing visitors toward the current novel experience.

### Implication

MyKhanWrites should function as:

> A refined author home that connects the writing projects without overwhelming visitors.

It should subtly link to ManTalksMedia, but not aggressively sell services.

---

## 2. Author Positioning

### Decision

The author positioning will be built around a layered identity:

- **Deep identity:** The Human Contradiction Writer
- **Public tone:** The Literary Shadow-Observer
- **Current doorway:** The Warm Worldbuilder

### Reasoning

The current novel is cozy literary fiction, but the author does not want to remain only in that genre. Other work includes:

- Neo-noir fiction
- Psychological thriller screenplay
- Science fiction
- Fantasy
- Future interactive/game-related storytelling
- Screenwriting

A genre-specific identity such as “cozy literary fiction novelist” would be too narrow and could trap the author’s future brand.

The better approach is to position the author around recurring creative concerns rather than genre. The recurring elements identified were:

- Loneliness and belonging
- Morally complicated people
- Hidden selves
- Comfort and darkness
- Inner worlds
- Cinematic/literary atmosphere

### Working Positioning Statement

> Meher Yar Khan writes literary and cinematic stories about loneliness, identity, and morally complicated people caught between comfort and darkness.

### Implication

The site should not say “I write many genres.”  
It should communicate a unified creative signature that can hold cozy fiction, noir, psychological thriller, speculative fiction, screenplays, and future interactive worlds.

---

## 3. Emotional Tone

### Decision

The emotional tone will be:

> **Warm Dusk with Quiet Noir beneath it.**

The approximate balance is:

- 60% warm dusk
- 30% quiet noir
- 10% strange rooms

### Reasoning

The site must avoid two opposite traps:

1. Becoming too cozy, which would misrepresent the author’s future range
2. Becoming too dark/noir, which would conflict with the upcoming cozy literary novel

The chosen tone creates a bridge between warmth and shadow. It supports the current novel while leaving room for darker, more morally complex work.

### Implication

The website should feel:

- Literary
- Warm
- Intimate
- Reflective
- Cinematic
- Slightly mysterious
- Serious but not pretentious

It should not feel like:

- A café website
- A crime thriller landing page
- A generic author blog
- A tech founder portfolio

---

## 4. Homepage Metaphor

### Decision

The core homepage metaphor will be:

> **A warm room, a dark window, and a steaming coffee mug beside unfinished pages.**

Later, the paper crane motif was added as a subtle symbolic object.

### Reasoning

The warm room and dark window express the central brand tension: comfort and darkness.

The coffee mug adds a human ritual. It suggests reflection, thought, solitude, and the presence of a writer just outside the frame.

The unfinished pages reinforce craft, writing, and story-in-progress.

The metaphor supports introspection without becoming too literal or cliché.

### Implication

The homepage visual should suggest:

- Deep thought
- Solitude
- Literary work
- Warmth
- Hidden emotional darkness
- Story-making

The coffee mug should be a small atmospheric detail, not the brand itself.

---

## 5. Paper Crane Motif

### Decision

The origami paper crane will be used as an **author-wide motif**.

The little girl in the yellow raincoat will remain **novel-specific** and should not become a general MyKhanWrites motif.

### Reasoning

The cozy literary fiction novel includes recurring motifs:

- Origami paper crane
- Little girl in a yellow raincoat

The paper crane is flexible enough for the author brand because it can symbolize:

- Fragility
- Memory
- Transformation
- Story-making
- Handmade craft
- Quiet hope
- Folding and shaping rough material into meaning

The yellow raincoat is more specific to the novel. Using it across the author hub could make MyKhanWrites feel too dependent on one book.

### Implication

The paper crane can appear:

- Near the unfinished pages in the hero image
- As a tiny logo/motif
- As a section divider
- Near the newsletter section
- As a subtle paper-fold detail in cards or textures

It should not become cute, oversized, colorful, mascot-like, or overused.

## 6. Site Structure

### Decision

The launch navigation will be minimal:

- Home
- Novel
- Writing
- About

Newsletter/contact will appear inside pages and the footer, not as primary navigation items.

### Reasoning

Early author websites often become bloated because the author tries to prove seriousness by showing everything. The chosen structure is intentionally restrained.

The visitor should quickly understand:

1. Who Meher Yar Khan is as a writer
2. What the current novel is
3. That the author has broader creative range
4. How to stay connected or make contact

### Implication

The site should not include major launch navigation for:

- Every unfinished book
- Every screenplay
- The writing app
- YouTube
- Apps
- Services
- ManTalksMedia

Those can be referenced subtly where relevant.

## 7. Homepage Hierarchy

### Decision

The homepage hero should lead with the **current novel doorway**, not a generic author statement.

### Reasoning

Because the upcoming novel is nearly complete and planned for launch soon, the homepage must serve the current launch priority.

However, the site is still an author hub, so the hero should weave the author identity into the current novel presentation.

The corrected hierarchy is:

1. Current novel doorway in the hero
2. Author positioning immediately after
3. Current novel expanded section
4. Writing range
5. About preview
6. Newsletter/contact
7. Footer links

### Implication

The homepage should not duplicate the immersive novel website. It should tease and direct.

The immersive novel site should absorb visitors into the deeper experience and handle the strongest email capture around the book.

## 8. Project Hierarchy

### Decision

The project hierarchy will be:

### Tier 1 — Public Focus

The upcoming cozy literary fiction novel.

### Tier 2 — Author Range

Fiction and screenwriting.

### Tier 3 — Quiet Background

Writing app, ManTalksMedia, YouTube, other apps, and future game ideas.

### Reasoning

Unfinished projects should be treated as signals of range, not as public destinations.

- Screen

### Reasoning

## 11. Visual Identity

### Decision

The visual style will be:

> **Dreamlike semi-realism with touches of cinematic realism.**

The site will use a balanced literary visual system, closer to typographic restraint than full immersion.

### Reasoning

Pure realism risks looking like stock photography.  
Pure abstraction risks lacking emotional connection.  
Heavy illustration or surrealism could make the site feel too fantasy-specific.

Dreamlike semi-realism allows the site to feel grounded, literary, and memorable.

### Implication

The visual system should include:

- Warm room
- Dark window
- Soft lamplight
- Steaming coffee mug
- Unfinished handwritten pages
- Subtle origami paper crane
- Paper textures
- Soft shadows
- Refined typography

It should avoid:

- Café branding
- Thriller clichés
- Cartoon style
- Overly cozy aesthetics
- Overly literal author portrait imagery

---

## 12. Animation and Interaction Style

### Decision

The interaction style will be:

> **Quietly alive, with a strict ceiling.**

### Reasoning

Motion should reinforce the author identity, not distract from it.

The site should feel atmospheric, not gimmicky. It is a literary author site, not an experimental design portfolio.

### Allowed Motion

- Slow steam from the mug
- Soft lamp glow
- Gentle text fades
- Paper-like card hover
- Subtle section reveals

### Avoided Motion

- Heavy parallax
- Video hero
- Glitch effects
- Dramatic noir transitions
- Animated cursor gimmicks
- Typewriter effects everywhere
- Scroll-jacking

### Implication

The hero should likely use a static/semi-real illustrated base image with subtle CSS atmospheric animation layered on top.

Motion should respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.

## 13. Newsletter Strategy

### Decision

The newsletter strategy will be:

> **Author letters, with a little behind-the-scenes creative journal.**

### Reasoning

The immersive novel site should remain the main email capture experience for the current novel.

MyKhanWrites should have a softer, broader author connection for readers interested in the writer beyond a single book.

No aggressive popups.  
No fake urgency.

## 17. Technical Build Direction

### Decision

The recommended build is:

> **Astro + Tailwind CSS**, deployed on Hostinger.

### Reasoning

The user has a Hostinger Business account and has already deployed Node.js and WordPress sites.

Hostinger supports Node.js apps and WordPress, but WordPress is not recommended for this project because the site requires a precise custom literary mood, tight performance, and restrained animation.

Astro is a strong fit because MyKhanWrites is mostly static/content-driven and should be fast, elegant, and easy to maintain.

### Recommended Stack

- Astro
- Tailwind CSS
- Minimal JavaScript
- CSS-only steam/lamp animations
- Static pages at launch
- Optional MDX later for letters/updates
- Newsletter embed/API
- Responsive optimized images
- `prefers-reduced-motion` support

### Avoid

- Hostinger AI builder
- WordPress for this specific site
- Heavy React app unless needed later
- Custom CMS at launch
- Video hero
- Overbuilt backend

### Implication

The build should be modern and polished but disciplined.

The technical goal:

> Beautiful, fast, maintainable, and emotionally precise.

It should make Meher Yar Khan feel like a serious literary and cinematic storyteller without trapping him in one genre.

The site should lead with the upcoming cozy literary fiction novel because it is the current launch priority, but the deeper author identity should remain broad enough to include noir, psychological thriller, speculative fiction, screenplays, and future interactive story worlds.

The strongest current identity is:

> A writer of loneliness, hidden selves, belonging, and morally complicated people caught between comfort and darkness.

The strongest visual metaphor is:

> A warm room, a dark window, a steaming mug, unfinished pages, and a small paper crane.
