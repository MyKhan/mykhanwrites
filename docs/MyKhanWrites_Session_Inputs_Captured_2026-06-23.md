## A. Premise Interview — answers captured

- **Q1 — The engine:** Strangers / visitors who are **stuck in life** appear when it rains, and
  someone notices the pattern.
- **Q2 — The protagonist:** A ~35-year-old **barista** who notices the platform and its visitors and
  believes he is *just an observer* — but turns out to have **been a visitor all along.**
- **Q3 — The emotional core:** People stuck in life — afraid of a decision, regretting something, or
  missing something — who don't get direct answers at the platform but leave with a **sense of
  direction.** The barista carries **buried trauma he doesn't remember;** he thinks he only watches
  others' grief and loneliness, then slowly realizes he has his own to face, and the platform lets him
  **come to terms with it.** → Core = *being stuck and finding direction; regret, buried grief, and
  quietly coming to terms with yourself.* (Warm at the center, real pain underneath.)
- **Q4 — The world:** A **liminal train-station platform** that appears only when it rains **and when
  someone is in need of reflection.**
- **Q5 — The boundary (do NOT reveal in public copy):**
  - The meaning of the **paper cranes**
  - Who the **girl in the yellow raincoat** is
  - The **ending**
  - The **barista's twist** (that he's a visitor with buried trauma) — *recommended hidden and
    accepted.* Public copy MAY hint he "believes he's only there to watch" as intrigue, never confirm.
- **Q6 — Comps:** Hasn't read the suggested comps. Initial inspiration was **_I Had That Same Dream
  Again_ (Yoru Sumino).** Signal for wording: gentle literary fiction, warm with melancholy, a light
  magical-realist touch.

**Genre read (for copy):** a quiet, contemplative novel in the vein of Japanese "healing fiction"
— warm at the center, melancholy and faintly uncanny at the edges. **Public approach: describe the
feeling, don't label; let the comps signal the shelf.**
