## 0. What Changed in V2

This V2 brief revises the previous Final Strategy + Build Brief after a second external review.

The second review did **not** require a strategic restart. It mostly recommended simplification, prioritization, and more operational clarity.

### Main V2 Changes

1. **Email strategy simplified**
   - Changed from two separate operational lists to one master audience with tags/segments.
   - Preserves separate audience intent without creating operational mess.

2. **Homepage hero clarified**
   - Homepage remains author-led but novel-aware.
   - It should not become a full novel landing page.
   - It should not send visitors away before they understand the author.

3. **Positioning tightened**
   - Removed “fiction” from the main positioning line so it can support fiction, screen, and other story formats.
   - Kept “warm stories with cold corners” as a strong but testable phrase.

4. **Tone simplified**
   - Reduced the tone vocabulary to fewer usable design instructions.

5. **Writing section weighted**
   - Fiction should receive the most weight.
   - Screen should be secondary.
   - Other Worlds should be treated as a light forward-looking note, not an equal mature pillar.

6. **Trust signals prioritized**
   - Prioritized real, public-facing credibility signals.
   - Removed weaker “unfinished work” signals from the main credibility layer.

7. **Contact copy strengthened**
   - Replaced passive category-heavy wording with clearer, more useful copy.

8. **Success metrics added**
   - Defined what the site should be judged by.

9. **Technical implementation clarified**
   - Added required decisions for analytics, contact backend, email platform, Open Graph, image handling, accessibility, and deployment.

## 1. Executive Summary

MyKhanWrites.com should be a focused, elegant, human author website for **Meher Yar Khan**.

It should support the upcoming novel launch while remaining a long-term author identity hub.

The site should not behave like:

- a commercial services website
- a generic author blog
- a cluttered personal archive
- a list of unfinished projects
- a duplicate of the immersive novel website
- a vague moodboard with no clear visitor action

It should behave like:

> A clear author home with atmosphere: a place where visitors understand who Meher is, what current story is opening next, and how to stay connected.

The governing principle is:

> **First make visitors understand. Then make them feel. Then invite them to act.**

### Reasoning

The earlier strategy had strong atmosphere but risked becoming product-thin and over-layered. This V2 version keeps the emotional identity while making the visitor path clearer.

---

## 2. Core Strategic Tension

The site must balance two truths:

1. The upcoming novel is the current launch priority.
2. MyKhanWrites must remain Meher’s long-term author home.

If the site becomes too novel-led, it stops being an author hub.

If it becomes too author-abstract, visitors may not understand what is happening now.

### Final Position

Use a **hybrid homepage**:

- Author-led
- Novel-aware
- Newsletter-focused
- Clear secondary path to the immersive novel site

### Reasoning

A visitor should not be sent to another website before understanding whose site this is and why they should care. But the novel is close to launch, so it must be visible immediately.

---

## 3. Author Positioning

### Final Public Direction

Use this as the leading positioning direction:

> **Meher Yar Khan writes warm stories with cold corners — about lonely people, hidden selves, and the places they go to feel less alone.**

Optional supporting range line:

> Across fiction, screen, and other worlds.

### Why This Replaced the Earlier Version

Earlier wording:

> Meher Yar Khan writes literary and cinematic stories about loneliness, identity, and morally complicated people caught between comfort and darkness.

That line was strategically accurate but too abstract and polished. It sounded more like positioning copy than a human author voice.

The revised line is stronger because it:

- is easier to understand
- has a memorable contrast
- avoids overusing abstract nouns
- supports cozy work and darker future work
- does not lock Meher into prose fiction only

### Watchout

“Warm stories with cold corners” is good, but it is slightly stylized. It should be tested in final copy. If it feels too clever in context, use a simpler version:

> Meher Yar Khan writes stories about lonely people, hidden selves, and the places they go to feel less alone.

### Internal-Only Identity Notes

The earlier framework remains useful internally:

- Human Contradiction Writer
- Literary Shadow-Observer
- Warm Worldbuilder

But these labels should not be used visibly on the website or treated as public-facing copy.

### Reasoning

The internal labels helped identify the creative spine, but public visitors need simple, human language.

## 4. Emotional Tone

### Final Tone Instruction

The site should feel:

- warm
- reflective
- quietly shadowed
- serious but human

It should not feel:

- cozy-café
- corporate
- thriller cliché
- precious
- overbuilt

### Reasoning

The earlier tone system used too many adjectives and ratios. A designer or AI builder needs fewer, clearer constraints. This simpler tone language is easier to execute.

## 5. Homepage Strategy

### Final Recommendation

The homepage should be **author-led but novel-aware**.

The hero should communicate:

1. This is Meher Yar Khan.
2. He writes warm, reflective stories with deeper shadows.
3. A current novel is nearing launch.
4. Visitors can join the author list or enter the novel world.

### Above-the-Fold Priorities

1. Author name
2. Humanized positioning line
3. Short current-novel mention
4. Primary CTA: subscribe / join author list
5. Secondary CTA: enter or visit novel site
6. Atmospheric visual that supports the author identity

### Recommended CTA Structure

**Primary CTA:**  
Join the Author List / Receive Letters / Join for Updates

**Secondary CTA:**  
Enter the Novel World / Visit the Novel Site

**Optional tertiary link:**  
Meet the Author

### Reasoning

The first CTA should build a direct relationship with the author. The novel-site CTA remains important, but should not be the only strong action above the fold.

The previous concern was that the homepage became “a front door to another door.” This revised structure fixes that by keeping the visitor oriented on MyKhanWrites first.

MyKhanWrites is the author home. The immersive novel site is the book-world experience. They should feel connected but not redundant.

## 8. Email and Newsletter Strategy

### Final Direction

Use **one master email audience** with tags/segments, not two entirely separate operational lists.

### Audience Logic

There are still two psychological entry paths:

1. **Author-site subscribers**
   - Interested in Meher as a writer
   - Open to future projects beyond the current novel

2. **Novel-site subscribers**
   - Interested mainly in the current novel or its world
   - May or may not want broader author updates

### Operational Setup

Use one email platform with tags such as:

- `source: mykhanwrites`
- `source: novel-site`
- `interest: author-updates`
- `interest: novel-launch`

### Cross-Invitation Strategy

Novel-site subscribers can later be invited to receive broader author letters.

Author-site subscribers can receive important novel launch updates without being treated as only novel-specific readers.

### Reasoning

This preserves Meher’s psychological distinction while avoiding the operational complexity of two fully separate lists.

Early audiences are usually small. Splitting them too aggressively too soon weakens the list and increases maintenance work.

## 9. Newsletter Copy Direction

The newsletter should not sound apologetic.

Avoid:

> sent quietly, when there is something worth saying

That is tasteful but too passive.

### Better Author-List Copy Direction

> Receive occasional notes from Meher Yar Khan on fiction, drafts, story worlds, and the long road from first idea to finished book.

### Stronger Launch-Aware Version

> I’m preparing my upcoming novel for launch. Join the author list for book news, behind-the-scenes notes, and occasional letters from the desk where the stories take shape.

### Reasoning

The newsletter is one of the most valuable pre-launch assets. It should feel calm and literary, but still give the visitor a clear reason to subscribe.

## 10. Visual Identity

### Final Direction

The visual identity should use:

> Warm room, dark window, lamplight, unfinished pages, subtle mug, and an origami paper crane.

### Mug Decision

The mug remains because Meher likes the generated hero images and it supports the emotional mood of introspection and reflection.

But the mug must remain atmosphere, not brand.

Use it as:

- a small object
- part of the desk scene
- a signal of reflection and human ritual

Do not use it as:

- a logo
- repeated icon
- major section motif
- café aesthetic

### Paper Crane Decision

The paper crane remains the stronger author-wide motif.

It can symbolize:

- story-making
- fragility
- memory
- transformation
- handmade craft
- quiet hope
- folding rough material into form

### Yellow Raincoat Decision

The yellow raincoat should remain novel-specific.

It may appear on:

- Novel page
- immersive novel site
- book-specific imagery
- subtle visual hint if needed

It should not become the overall MyKhanWrites motif.

### Reasoning

The mug is emotionally accessible but familiar. The paper crane is more distinctive. The yellow raincoat is powerful but belongs mainly to the current novel.

## 12. Writing Page Strategy

### Final Direction

Use these categories:

- Fiction
- Screen
- Other Worlds

But do not give them equal weight.

### Recommended Layout

**Fiction**  
Largest card or primary block.

**Screen**  
Smaller secondary card.

**Other Worlds**  
Small forward-looking note, not a full equal pillar.

### Category Descriptions

**Fiction**  
Novels and prose stories about lonely people, hidden selves, and the places that comfort or unsettle them.

**Screen**  
Cinematic stories shaped by psychological tension, moral pressure, and the things people refuse to face.

**Other Worlds**  
Future experiments in interactive and immersive storytelling, developed slowly and only when the story needs another form.

### Guardrail

Do not list every unpublished or unfinished project at launch.

Do not use the Writing page to prove productivity.

Use it to show direction and range.

### Reasoning

Equal-weight cards could imply that Fiction, Screen, and Other Worlds are equally developed bodies of work. That may not be true yet. Weighting them honestly creates more trust.

## 14. Trust Signals

### Final Direction

Use honest, high-signal trust markers.

### Prioritized Trust Signals

1. **Author photo**
   - Makes the author feel real quickly.

2. **Current novel launch window**
   - Shows something concrete is happening.

3. **Writing tool link**
   - Proves Meher builds and ships creative infrastructure.

4. **Early reader quote or beta comment**
   - Only if available and honest.

5. **Completed screenplay or other work**
   - Mention carefully, preferably in About/Writing, not as a boast.

### Avoid as Primary Trust Signals

- “Other drafts”
- “Books still needing editing”
- vague future projects
- too many unfinished works
- grand claims without public proof

### Reasoning

Some information that feels impressive to the author can read as unfinished-pile evidence to visitors. Trust signals should make the author feel real and active, not scattered.

## 17. Animation Strategy

### Final Direction

The site should feel:

> Quietly alive, with a strict ceiling.

Allowed:

- subtle steam movement
- soft lamp glow
- gentle fade-ins
- paper-like hover effects
- subtle section reveals

Avoid:

- heavy parallax
- scroll-jacking
- animated cursor
- glitch effects
- dramatic noir transitions
- typewriter effects everywhere
- autoplay video

### Accessibility Requirement

All non-essential motion should respect reduced-motion preferences.

### Reasoning

Motion should support atmosphere, not become the site’s personality.

## 22. Final Build Principles

1. Do not overbuild.
2. Do not make the site a personal archive.
3. Do not hide the current novel behind mood.
4. Do not let the current novel swallow the author identity.
5. Do not turn the writing tool into the main story.
6. Do not list unfinished projects as proof.
7. Do not use animation as decoration.
8. Do not make the mug the brand.
9. Do not split the email audience too early.
10. Do make the site clear, warm, credible, and easy to act on.
