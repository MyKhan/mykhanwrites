Meher Yar Khan is a Pakistani-Finnish writer and digital nomad. His first novel, *They Only Came When It Rained* — a quiet, contemplative story about people who can't move on — arrives in August 2026. By training he's an electrical and software engineer, and he builds writing tools, including Compare My Drafts, a web app for taking a draft from a messy first pass to a finished one.

## A note from me

I owe the reading habit to my grandfather. It started with *Goosebumps*, ran straight into fantasy and science fiction, and never really stopped — Pratchett, Gaiman, Tolkien, and the self-published worlds of Steven Campbell's *Hard Luck Hank*, which I mostly listen to now, because audiobooks are how a busy life still gets to read.

I wrote my first story when I was ten: a different monster on every page, one after another, until I ran out of paper. I've written several manuscripts since. None of them found a traditional publisher, so I'm putting this one into the world myself — which feels, finally, like the right way to do it.

I write between countries, usually with a coffee going. It's probably no accident that this first novel ended up living in a café.
